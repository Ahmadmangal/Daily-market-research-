**Date:** October 30, 2025  

**Market Recap (for October 29, 2025):**  
The U.S. markets traded mixed ahead of the Federal Reserve’s policy decision.  
- **S&P 500:** -0.28%  
- **Nasdaq 100:** +0.41%  
- **Dow Jones Industrial Average:** -0.16%  

**Commodities:**  
- **Gold:** +0.6%  
- **Silver:** +0.4%  

Gold and silver gained modestly as investors awaited the FOMC outcome, supported by a slightly weaker dollar and lower real yields.

**Federal Reserve (FOMC) Update:**  
The Fed kept its benchmark rate unchanged at 5.25%–5.50%.  
Chair Powell maintained a “higher for longer” tone, noting inflation progress remains limited.  
QT (quantitative tightening) continues as scheduled, while Powell hinted that higher bond yields are already tightening financial conditions — possibly reducing the need for further hikes.  

**Summary:**  
Markets remained cautious, tech outperformed slightly, and precious metals gained.  
Investors are now pricing potential rate cuts for early 2026 if inflation keeps cooling.
